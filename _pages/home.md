I'm a computational immunologist at the Warren Lab in the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center, where I'm part of an amazing team working to understand T-cell responses to pathogen-associated malignancies. I obtained my Ph.D. in Bioinformatics from Georgia Tech, where I developed scalable computational methods to understand anti-malarial drug resistance in Plasmodium falciparum. During my Ph.D., I had the privilege of being part of a project that assembled the first complete genome for the malaria-causing parasite Plasmodium brasilianum.

When I'm not working on research, I love playing board games, travelling, and practicing bonsai, which has been my recent obsession. I also enjoy exploring the great outdoors by hiking, biking, and kayaking, and relaxing with some video games. 

Currently, my research interests focus on using graph-based machine learning techniques to identify likely pathogen-associated T-cells from large-scale AIRR-Seq and scRNA-Seq datasets. It's a cutting-edge area of research that's both challenging and exciting.
