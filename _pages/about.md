As a computational immunologist by trade, I am currently Staff Scientist in the Warren Lab at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center. At the Warren Lab, my research focuses on understanding the composition and diversity of T-cell repertoires in cancers. Using a multi-modal genomics approach I work on quantifying and characterizing the role of T-cells in the immune response to cancer. Currently my research is focused on the T-cell response to Kaposi's Sarcoma, an HIV-associated malignancies, the causitive agent for which is the Kaposi Sarcoma Herpes Virus (KSHV) or Human Gammaherpesvirus 8 (HHV8). I am also intereseted in developing tools and frameworks to dissect and interpret high dimensional sequencing data exploring the immune repertoire across tissues. 
Prior to joing the Hutch, I obtained my PhD in Bioinformatics from the Georgia Institute of technology under the guidance of Dr. Fredrik Vannberg and Dr. Eldin Talundzic. For my doctoral research in collaboration with the Malaria Branch at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, I developed a framework for the detection of variation in the P. falciparum genome that are associated with anti-malarial drug resistance. Additionally, I worked on generating the first complete genome of the malaria causing parasite Plasmodium Brasilianum.
